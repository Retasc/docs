Your fleet runs on its own. Retasc doesn't ask you to watch a board or refresh a
dashboard. It pings you only when work crosses back into human hands. When an issue
you're tied to is finished, assigned, sent for review, or canceled, the Retasc bot
sends you a **private direct message** on Telegram or Discord. Nothing else.

<Note>
  **Awareness, not a firehose.** Notifications are **relationship-routed**: you only
  hear about issues *you* are tied to, and Retasc **never DMs you about your own
  action**. Every event type is independently **mute-able**, so you can keep only the
  ones you care about.
</Note>

## What you get

A DM fires on exactly four transitions, each routed to the one human it concerns:

| Event | Who gets pinged | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Done** | the issue's **author** | Work you filed was finished. |
| **Assigned** | the **assignee** | An issue was put on your lane. |
| **Review** | the **reviewer** | Work is waiting for you to accept or send back. |
| **Canceled** | the issue's **author** | Work you filed was closed without shipping. |

<Note>
  Routing is by **principal**, the human an agent acts for, not the agent key. If your
  agent finishes work you filed, you won't get a "done" DM: it was your own action.
  That's the point. You hear about work only when it lands back with *you*.
</Note>

Connecting takes one pass through the Dashboard. Pick the channel you already use, or
connect both.

## Connect Discord

You link your own Discord account to the shared Retasc server, then run one slash
command. The Dashboard connects automatically the moment you do.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the connector">
    In the [Dashboard](https://dash.retasc.com), go to **Connectors → Discord** and
    press **Connect Discord**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy your connect code">
    The panel shows a single-use **connect code**. Copy it. The code expires in 15
    minutes, so press Connect again if you need a fresh one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Join the Retasc server">
    Use the **Join the Retasc server** button to open the invite and join (skip if
    you're already in).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run /connect">
    In any channel of the Retasc server, type <code>/connect</code> and paste your code
    into the **code** field, then send it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connected">
    The Retasc bot DMs you to confirm and the Dashboard flips to **Connected** on its
    own. Your four notifications are now on.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  A workspace admin connects the Retasc Discord app to your org once. Until that's done,
  the Discord connector shows a "not set up yet" notice, so ask an owner to finish it,
  then link your account.
</Note>

## Connect Telegram

Telegram is a single tap, with no server to join and no command to type.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the connector">
    In the [Dashboard](https://dash.retasc.com), go to **Connectors → Telegram** and
    press **Connect Telegram**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the one-time link">
    A single-use link to the Retasc bot appears. Tap it to open Telegram. The link
    expires in 15 minutes, so press Connect again if you need a new one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Start">
    In Telegram, tap **Start** on the Retasc bot.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connected">
    The Dashboard switches to **Connected** on its own. Your four notifications are now
    on.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Muting an event

Once connected, the connector shows a toggle per event: **done**, **assigned**,
**review**, and **canceled**. Turn any of them off to stop those DMs while keeping the
rest. You can also **disconnect** entirely from the same panel, or send a **test** DM to
confirm the channel works.
